Mental Health Questionnaires

Conduct mental health questionnaires with the addition of emotional recognition technology.

PHQ-9 Questionnaire

The PHQ-9 questionnaire is a mental health self-screening questionnaire which is designed to determine if an indivudal is exhibiting symptoms consistent with depression disorder.

The questionnaire will ask 9 different questions about depressive symptoms. Depending on the answer selected a value will be assigned to the answer between 0 and 3. Once all questions have been answered a score will then be calculated at the end of the questionnaire which will determine depression severeity. The maximum score attainable is 27, meaning this score correlates to the most severe level of depression. In addition to the questionnaires score itself an emotional metric will be given based off of data captured by the emotional recognition model during the questionnaire.

The PHQ-9 questionnaire is a proven psychological screening method for depression. It was first proposed by psychologists Kroenke, Spitzer, Williams in psychological academic paper (2001). Since then it has proved an invaluable asset in depression diagnosis.

GAD-7 Questionnaire

The GAD-7 questionnaire is a mental health self-screening questionnaire which is designed to determine if an indvidual is exhibiting symptoms consistent with generalised anxiety disorder (GAD).

The questionnaire asks 7 different questions relating to GAD symptoms. Depending on the answer selected a value will be assigned to the answer between 0 and 3. Once all questions have been asnwered a score will then be calculated at the end of the questionanire which will determine the severity of generalised anxity disorder. The maximum score attainable is 21, meaning this score correlates to the most severe level of generalised anxiety disorder. In addition to the questionnaires score itself an emotional metric will be given based off of data captured by the emotional recognition model during the questionnaire

The GAD-7 questionnaire is a proven psychological screening method for generalised anxiety disorder. It was first proposed by psychologists Spitzer, Kroenke, Williams, and Lowe in a psychological academic paper (2006). Since then it has proved an invaluable asset in GAD diagnosis.
